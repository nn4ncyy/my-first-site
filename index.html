my favourite book

No Longer Human by Osamu Dazai

My favourite genre of books is literary fiction, and this book falls in that category. No Longer Human follows a protagonist who is alienated from society, the original Japanese title is literally translated as "disqualified from being human".

This is my current favourite book because more than any horror literature, the way Dazai writes about the human experice of desolate isolation is terrifying. It so greatly delves into the human psyche and explores the scary descent into self destruction.

Us and Them (2018)

My rating: 10/10

Us and Them follows a couple and their trials and tribulations that ultimately causes them to drift apart. Some years later, they run into each other and reminisce on what went wrong.
This is one of my favourite movies becauses it depicts the realities of life and the realities of relationships. It depicts the rawness of love and human emotion.

La Haine (1995)

My rating: 10/10

This movie is a masterpiece when it comes to cinematography, pacing, concepts, casting, and plot. It deals with major themes of class inequality, racism, police brutality, and companionship. It breaks down class through art while perfectly portraying the unique experience of youth culture.
La Haine is imaginary, brutal, honest, harsh, and so, so meaningful.

Everything Everywhere All At Once (2022)

My rating: 10/10

Children of immigrants will understand what I'm saying when I say this movie heartwrenching. This movie leaves me actually gobsmacked as it deals with themes of optimistic nihilism mixed with intergenerational trauma in an explosive and meticulously crafted masterpiece. It's funny, sad, action-packed, confusing, beautiful, and hopeful.
